Business Context

Customer churn is a critical problem for subscription-based businesses, as acquiring new customers is significantly more expensive than retaining existing ones. This project simulates a real-world scenario where a company wants to understand why customers leave and identify early warning signals to improve retention strategies.

Business Questions

The analysis focused on answering the following key questions:

  • Which customer segments have the highest churn rate?
  • Does customer tenure influence the likelihood of churn?
  • Are short-tenure customers more likely to leave?
  • Which behavioral or account-level factors are most associated with churn?

Dataset Overview

The dataset contains customer-level information including tenure, service usage, contract type, and churn status. Data cleaning and preprocessing were performed using Microsoft Excel, followed by exploratory analysis to identify patterns and trends.

Methodology

The analysis followed a structured data analysis workflow:

  • Data cleaning and validation
  • Exploratory data analysis using pivot tables
  • Segmentation based on tenure groups
  • Visualization of churn patterns using charts

The goal was not model complexity, but interpretability and business relevance.

  • A large proportion of churn occurred before customers fully adopted the service.

Business Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following actions are recommended:

  • Implement stronger onboarding and support programs for customers in their first 12 months.
  • Monitor early engagement metrics to identify at-risk users.
  • Introduce targeted retention campaigns for new customers.

Limitations & Next Steps

This analysis was limited to exploratory techniques and did not include predictive modeling. Future work could include building churn prediction models using logistic regression or tree-based methods, as well as validating findings with larger datasets.
